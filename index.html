Choosing Accounting Software

What actually matters when making the decision

As an accounting system provider, we are often asked what the most important feature is when evaluating accounting software. Most people expect a technical answer — features, reports, dashboards.

But the real answer is simpler, and often overlooked: development capability.

The real value of a system is not what it does today, but what it allows you to build tomorrow.

Most accounting platforms are designed to cover around 80 to 85 per cent of business needs straight out of the box.

The remaining gap is intentional. It is where customization happens.

Good software is not complete — it is adaptable.
HOW IT USED TO WORK

In the past, adapting software meant modifying the source code directly.

Months of development time
High costs
High technical dependency
No safe upgrade path

Once modified, systems became difficult — or impossible — to upgrade.

Many companies ended up locked into outdated versions, unable to evolve.

HOW IT WORKS TODAY

Modern accounting systems follow a different philosophy.

Instead of modifying the core, they provide built-in development tools that allow extensions without breaking the system.

Faster customization
Lower costs
Cleaner architecture
Upgrades without losing changes
Flexibility is no longer a workaround — it is part of the product design.

This approach keeps the core system clean and efficient, while still allowing businesses to adapt it to their specific needs.

It also explains why development capability is no longer a secondary feature.

It is the feature that determines whether your software will still work for you in five years.

So if you are evaluating accounting software, do not just look at what it can do today.

Look at what it will let you become.
